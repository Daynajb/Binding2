Colophon

How changing the way we see will change the way we make.

These texts suggest new ways of looking at design history and theory to inform our practice and create more interesting work in response. Can We Teach Graphic Design History Without Chronology? Looks at the ways we interpret history as a whole in a neat and linear fashion that often cuts out the humanity and sprawling nature of ideas. Aggie toppins points out that much of the value of design movements is lost when we cut it up into neat, chronological strips. We Need Graphic Design Histories That Look Beyond the Profession posits that the way that we look at design history now is restrictive. International Students Can Teach Us A Lot About Design Education for All highlights the value in considered what design means across cultures and asks the user to consider different audiences. By considering who may be viewing our work across different cultural contexts we can creat more diverse and inclusive works. All of these texts urges the designer to consider different ways of looking to enrich their practices. I think designers can become consumed by the monoculture and forget the massive array of perspectives that exist.
